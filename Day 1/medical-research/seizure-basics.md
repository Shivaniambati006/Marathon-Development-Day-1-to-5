# Seizures: Overview

## What is a Seizure?

A seizure is a sudden, uncontrolled electrical disturbance in the brain. This abnormal activity can affect movement, behavior, awareness, and body functions. Seizures can last from a few seconds to a few minutes depending on the type. 

Seizures occur when groups of brain cells send abnormal electrical signals. These signals can cause involuntary movements, muscle stiffness, or loss of consciousness.

---

# Types of Seizures

Seizures are mainly classified into two major categories.

## 1. Focal (Partial) Seizures

Focal seizures **start in one specific area of the brain**.

### Types of Focal Seizures

- **Focal Aware Seizure**
  - The person remains conscious.

- **Focal Impaired Awareness Seizure**
  - The person becomes confused or unaware of their surroundings.

- **Focal Seizure with Motor Symptoms**
  - Jerking movements in one part of the body.

- **Focal Seizure without Motor Symptoms**
  - Unusual sensations such as strange smells, feelings, or visual disturbances.

---

## 2. Generalized Seizures

Generalized seizures **start on both sides of the brain at the same time**.

### Types of Generalized Seizures

- **Tonic-Clonic Seizure**
  - Body becomes stiff (tonic phase) and then begins shaking (clonic phase).
  - This is the **most commonly recognized seizure type**.

- **Absence Seizure**
  - Brief staring spells with loss of awareness for a few seconds.

- **Tonic Seizure**
  - Muscles suddenly become stiff.

- **Clonic Seizure**
  - Repeated jerking movements.

- **Myoclonic Seizure**
  - Sudden, quick muscle jerks.

- **Atonic Seizure**
  - Sudden loss of muscle control, which may cause the person to fall.

---

# Seizure vs Epilepsy

It is common to confuse **seizures** with **epilepsy**, but they are not the same thing.

## Seizure

- A **single event**.
- Occurs due to sudden abnormal electrical activity in the brain.
- Can be caused by:
  - Fever
  - Head injury
  - Low blood sugar
  - Infection
  - Lack of oxygen to the brain
- A person may have **one seizure and never experience another**.

## Epilepsy

- A **neurological disorder**.
- Diagnosed when a person experiences **repeated, unprovoked seizures**.
- Typically defined as **two or more seizures occurring over time**.
