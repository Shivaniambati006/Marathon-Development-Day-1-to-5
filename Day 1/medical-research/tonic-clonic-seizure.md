# Tonic-Clonic Seizure

A **tonic-clonic seizure** is one of the most common and severe types of seizures.  
It consists of two main phases: the **tonic phase** and the **clonic phase**.

## Tonic Phase
- The body suddenly becomes **stiff**.
- Muscles contract strongly.
- The person usually **loses consciousness**.
- This phase may cause the person to **fall to the ground**.

## Clonic Phase
- The muscles begin **rhythmic jerking movements**.
- These jerks can affect the **arms, legs, and entire body**.
- Breathing may become irregular during this stage.

Because the person loses control of their body, tonic-clonic seizures can be **dangerous due to the risk of injury**, especially if the person falls or hits nearby objects.

# Why This Topic Was Chosen

**Predicting and detecting seizures in real time is very challenging.**  
Seizures can occur suddenly and the body movements may vary from person to person.

Because of this, building a reliable **seizure detection or prediction system** is not easy. However, developing such systems is very important because they can:

- Alert caregivers or family members
- Provide faster medical assistance
- Reduce the risk of injury during seizures

Real-time detection systems can therefore play a major role in **preventing accidents and improving the safety of people living with epilepsy**.
